In a Nutshell

The United Kingdom was once an island up north, invaded by the Romans, the Saxons, the Vikings, then the Normans. The state shares a long history of monarchy and is currently being ruled by Queen Elizabeth II since 1952. Under the Treaty of Union, the United Kingdom became a unified state in 1707 as the kingdoms of England, Northern Island, Wales and Scotland came into union in a unitary state, the Great Britain. The United Kingdom did not only play a huge role in world history— they gave birth to Shakespeare and even the Beatles! They also are a huge leader in developing democracy and achieving state-of-the-art advancements in the field of arts, literature, and science.

Almost all of UK's history still exists today and it's everyone's favorite place to be! Explore UK's share of the world's masterpieces, evident on the strongly-built yet also intricate architecture that withstood the test of time.
